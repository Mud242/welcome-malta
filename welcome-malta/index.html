1 Job search — start before you fly

Malta's Public Employment Service is Jobsplus. Its vacancies also appear on the European EURES portal — so you can line everything up from home.

🏛️ Jobsplus — Maltese Public Employment Service

1
Register for a free account: click Sign in (top right) → Sign up / Login via Self-Registration.
2
EU nationals can register before moving — use your current home address.
3
Upload your CV, search & apply, receive daily vacancy updates; employers can match your profile to openings.
✈️ Moving without a job? You can register as unemployed with Jobsplus in your first days (first-time registrants guidance) — tell EURES Malta you're coming and they'll walk you through it.
Source: jobsplus.gov.mt · first-time registrants

🌐 The EURES portal

  • The same jobs as Jobsplus appear on eures.europa.eu — Search for a Job → country: Malta
  • Create a EURES CV profile so Maltese employers and EURES advisers can find you
  • Need more channels? Ask your EURES adviser — they can point you to reputable, licensed recruitment options in Malta

💶 Targeted Mobility Scheme — money for your move

The EURES Targeted Mobility Scheme (TMS) provides financial support to jobseekers moving for work and to employers hiring EU nationals — e.g. travel allowance, relocation support set by your country's TMS service, and help with integration after starting. Apply before starting the new job — you cannot claim retroactively. Ask the EURES adviser in your home country.

Source: eures.europa.eu — TMS · adviser finder: europa.eu/eures/portal

2 Papers in order: departure → work start → residence

The sequence below reflects how Maltese systems chain together. Do it in order and you avoid most back-and-forth.

📤 Before leaving home: social security & benefits

1
Get a U1 statement from your social security institution — it may be required in Malta for access to social assistance.
2
Transferring unemployment benefit? Apply for a U2 document before departure and present it in Malta after registering as unemployed (time-limited window — ask your EURES adviser).
3
Posted temporarily by your employer? Get an A1 form — it certifies which country's social security applies while in Malta.
EU coordination rules: Reg. (EC) 883/2004 · help: find a EURES adviser in your country

💶 Maltese Tax Identification Number (TIN)

1
Apply to the Commissioner for Revenue (CFR) — online or by form — to be entered in the tax register as an EU national taking up employment.
2
Documents: EU ID/passport, employment contract or offer, Maltese address if available.
3
Your employer then deducts income tax and SSC automatically at source under the Final Settlement System (FSS) — employees normally don't file a return.
💡 Tip: in person at CFR TaxPayer Services (Floriana) or online via cfr.gov.mt → Tax registration for EU citizens.
Source: cfr.gov.mt

🌍 Double taxation — check before you move

  • Inform your home tax department of your departure and destination before leaving.
  • Malta has an extensive double taxation treaty network (70+ countries); the treaty decides where your salary is taxed and prevents paying twice.
  • Once resident & employed in Malta, employment income is generally taxed in Malta at progressive resident rates (0–35%).
Source: cfr.gov.mt — Double Taxation Agreements

🔢 Social Security number (via Jobsplus)

1
Sign your contract. Your employer is legally obliged to register new employees with Jobsplus.
Deadline for employer: within ~4 working days of start
2
Registration creates your Social Security (SSC) number — driving Class 1 contributions (pension, sickness, healthcare).
3
Keep the confirmation. Ask your employer for the engagement confirmation for your records.
Source: jobsplus.gov.mt

🪪 eResidence document (stays over 3 months)

1
Apply online on the Identità Expatriates Portal — upon arrival with a job; you may apply in advance too.
2
Identità responds within ~48 working hours with your biometrics appointment, the form and the document list.
3
Attend biometrics with originals; your eResidence card is issued on your basis of residence (e.g. worker).
📎 Employment contract or Jobsplus registration
📎 Passport / EU ID card
📎 Maltese address
Sources: Expatriates Portal · identita.gov.mt

3 Qualifications & regulated professions

Skip this at your peril: starting before you move can save weeks of waiting in Malta.

📜 Regulated professions — warrant first

  • Nurses, teachers, architects, electricians, pharmacists… if your profession is regulated in Malta, you must first contact the national profession's body/council to apply for a warrant or register.
  • Only then may you practise — check the official list before accepting an offer.
Source: MFHEA — regulated professions & competent authorities

🎓 Recognition of qualifications (MQRIC)

  • Get your academic & vocational qualifications recognised — employers (and warranting bodies) often ask for it.
  • The process runs fully online via the Malta Qualifications Recognition Information Centre (MQRIC, under MFHEA).
  • Have scans of diplomas + transcripts + translations ready.
Apply: MQRIC online application

4 Opening a bank account

All EU residents have the legal right to a basic payment account (EU Directive 2014/92/EU). Maltese banks love paperwork — arrive prepared and the visit takes 30 minutes instead of 3 appointments.

📄 Usually requested documents

  • EU passport or national ID card
  • Proof of Maltese address — registered rental contract, or employer letter / recent utility bill
  • Employment contract or payslip
  • Maltese Tax ID number (many banks ask for it)
  • Some banks want a face-to-face appointment — book ahead
💡 Workaround: ask your new employer which bank handles payroll — onboarding there is often faster; a basic payment account must legally offer core services at the lowest fee tier.

🏦 Main retail banks in Malta

  • Bank of Valletta (BOV) — largest branch/ATM network
  • HSBC Malta
  • APS Bank
  • BNF Bank

💱 Smart bridge: multi-currency account (N26 / Revolut)

Many arrivals open a free multi-currency account (Revolut, N26) on day one: instant EUR/IBAN for rent, deposits and daily spending while the local bank processes your application. Payrolls: under the SEPA Regulation (EU 260/2012) an employer must accept any SEPA IBAN you give them — local ATM coverage is the only real argument for a Maltese bank.

5 Housing & the rental market

Rent is usually your biggest cost. The ranges below are an orientation snapshot, not live prices — always confirm on live portals.

Sliema / St Julian's / Gżira / Msida

€1,200+indicative · 1-bedroom/month
  • Highest prices, shortest commutes to work hubs
  • Strong demand — apartments go in days

Central: Mosta / Birkirkara / Naxxar

≈€800–1,100indicative · 1-bedroom/month
  • Good value; well connected by bus
  • Popular with commuters

North: St Paul's Bay / Qawra / Mellieħa

≈€850–1,100indicative · 1-bedroom/month
  • Touristic summer towns, quiet winters
  • Large expat communities

South: Żurrieq / Birżebbuġa / Marsascala

≈€700–950indicative · 1-bedroom/month
  • Cheapest per m² on the main island
  • Longer commutes to northern hubs

Gozo: Victoria / Marsalforn / Xagħra

≈€600–850indicative · 1-bedroom/month
  • Calmer pace, lower prices
  • Commute = ferry (~25 min from Ċirkewwa)

🔎 Live listings

  • Frank Salt · Alliance · Quicklets
  • Maltapark — large classifieds (incl. private landlords)

⚖️ Your rights as a tenant

  • Every rental contract must be registered by the landlord with the Housing Authority (Private Residential Leases Act, S.L. 604.01). Unregistered = on default statutory rates; get your copy.
  • Deposits are typically 1 month, held against damages/unpaid bills.
  • Agency fee, if an agent is used, is customarily ~half a month's rent from each side.
Source: housingauthority.gov.mt — rent registration

🧳 First days: temporary stay & setup

  • Book 1–3 weeks of temporary accommodation (hostel, aparthotel, Airbnb) — never commit to a long-term flat unseen.
  • SIM card: prepaid from Epic / GO / Melita — also sold at the airport.
  • Utilities: electricity & water run through ARMS and usually follow the registered lease; choose internet carefully — Melita/GO/Epic contracts often lock you in 18–24 months; ask for monthly options.
  • Home insurance: cheap contents cover protects that deposit argument later.

🚨 Rental-scam red flags

  • Price far below the ranges above for prime areas
  • "Send the deposit before viewing / I'm abroad, keys after payment"
  • Only WhatsApp contact, photo-only identity, no Housing Authority registration
Rule of thumb: No viewing → no payment. Pay through traceable channels and keep the registration copy.

6 Cost of living & climate

💸 Budget orientation

  • Rent dominates the budget (see ranges above)
  • Groceries & eating out: roughly in line with / below much of N-Europe
  • Transport: cheapest item — free resident buses
  • Utilities: watch A/C in August & heaters in damp January
Live crowdsourced comparison: Numbeo — Malta/Valletta

☀️ Climate

  • ~3,000 hours of sunshine a year — among Europe's highest
  • Summers hot & dry: ~30°C+, July–September
  • Winters mild but windy & humid: 10–17°C, rain Nov–Feb (pack a jacket — houses lack insulation/central heating)
  • Sea swimming: roughly April→November
Source: Malta Met Office

🧭 Good to know

  • Both English & Maltese are official — all services work in English
  • Tap water is safe but desalinated — many buy bottled/refill
  • Currency: Euro · Plugs: UK-style 3-pin (bring adapters)
  • Time zone: CET/CEST

7 Working conditions & pay

Maltese employment law applies equally to EU nationals — Employment and Industrial Relations Act (EIRA), enforced by the DIER.

💰 National minimum wage

€229.44/wk 2026 · whole-time, age 18+ · 17y: €222.66 · COLA €4.66/wk
Source: DIER

🏖️ Leave & hours

  • Standard full-time week: 40 h; overtime mostly 1.5×/2×
  • 25 working days annual leave (pro-rata part-year)
  • Public holidays on top (14–15 per year)

⏳ Probation & deductions

  • Probation: 6 months default (up to 1 year for higher-paid technical/managerial posts)
  • Tax + ~10% Class 1 social contributions are withheld automatically (FSS)
  • Gov't statutory bonus & weekly allowance are paid on top of wage
🆘 Problems at work? DIER (Dept. for Industrial & Employment Relations): freephone 153 · dier.gov.mt — confidential, free of charge.

8 Healthcare

🏥 How entitlement works for EU workers

  • European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) from your home country: emergency public healthcare in Malta, valid for your first ~3 months.
  • Once you start working & paying social security: you receive the same public healthcare benefits as residents — present your work contract + payslips (EU Reg. 883/2004).
  • Main hospital: Mater Dei (Msida); local health centres in towns; pharmacists handle minor things.
  • Private top-up insurance is popular & affordable — private GP visits ~€15–30 out-of-pocket.

📞 Numbers worth saving

112 Emergency — ambulance / police / fire
2545 3000 Mater Dei switchboard
Servizz.gov 2590 3000 — all government services in one line

9 Getting around

🚌 Free buses for residents

  • Residents entitled to free standard day & night fares with a Tallinja card — since 2026 you select a plan: MyTallinja Intro (free) or MyTallinja Basic (€14.50).
  • Card delivery ≈ 10 working days — apply as soon as you have Maltese residence.
Source: Malta Public Transport

⛴️ Gozo & water links

  • Gozo Channel ferry: Ċirkewwa ↔ Mġarr (~25 min)
  • Fast ferry Valletta → Gozo (foot passengers); Sliema & Three Cities ferries

🚗 Driving licence

  • EU licences are valid in Malta — drive on the LEFT
  • Want a Maltese licence? Exchange at Transport Malta — no test required for EU licences
  • Parking is the real boss fight in Sliema/Gżira
Source: transport.gov.mt

🛴 Alt transport

  • e-scooters restricted on arterial roads
  • Ride hailing: Bolt / Uber local operators
  • Bikes: improving lanes, still a brave choice

10 Family life & pets

🏫 Schools & childcare

  • State schools are free for residents; tuition is largely English-medium with Maltese also taught; enrol via the Ministry for Education (proof of residence + vaccination records).
  • Church schools (donation-based) and independent schools are alternatives — early application advised.
  • Free Childcare Scheme for children under school age when both parents work or study.
Source: education.gov.mt

🐾 Bringing pets

  • EU Pet Passport + ISO microchip + valid rabies vaccination (first jab: 21-day wait before travel)
  • Check airline/ferry rules early — some routes have limits
  • ⚠️ Pet-friendly rentals are scarce: secure landlord permission in writing before signing

🫶 Integration

  • Free Maltese & English courses (Jobsplus & community centres)
  • Active expat communities on every coast — especially St Paul's Bay, Sliema, Gozo
  • Local councils run integration events — ask in your locality
